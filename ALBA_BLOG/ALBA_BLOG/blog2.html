Beginner Tips for LEGO Creators

1. Research Thoroughly – Don’t Be a Brickhead

Before you start splurging, take a moment to do some detective work. You don’t want to end up with a castle when you were aiming for a spaceship! This hobby can add up quickly, be sure you’re going to be happy with your purchase.

2. Set a Budget – Don’t Let LEGO Rob Your Piggy Bank

Sure, LEGO sets are tempting, but your bank account will thank you later if you set a budget. Otherwise, you might end up trading your car for a Millennium Falcon. Or, if you are after a Millennium Falcon, make it a savings goal. The satisfaction once you reach your target will be amazing!

3. Focus on Themes – Don’t Get Lost in a LEGO Labyrinth

With so many themes to choose from, it’s easy to feel like a kid in a candy store. But remember, it’s better to master one theme than to dabble in everything like a LEGO buffet.

4. Stay Updated – Don’t Let LEGO Surprises Catch You Off Guard

LEGO announcements can be as surprising as finding a missing brick in your carpet. Stay updated to avoid the disappointment of missing out on that limited edition set. Keep an eye on the LEGO.com Coming Soon page for updates.

5. Quality Over Quantity – Don’t Build a LEGO Tower of Babel

It’s tempting to amass a tower of LEGO sets taller than Mount Everest, but remember, it’s quality over quantity. Your collection will thank you, and so will your storage space!
